STAGE 04-ATTACHMENT


Somewhere along the way, this stopped feeling like two people who happened to meet in an OPD.
The conversations felt lighter. The days felt a little more familiar. Without realising it, I had started keeping space for you in my routine.
You were no longer that senior junior interaction from December.
You had become a part of my day.
And the strange thing was… nothing big had happened.
No dramatic moment. No sudden confession. No movie scene.
Just time.
Time spent talking. Time spent understanding. Time quietly doing what it does best — bringing two people closer.
This was the stage where I still didn’t know what to call it.
But I knew something had changed.
Because your presence had started mattering.
And somewhere inside all those ordinary moments…
the idea of you had become special.
Somewhere between the conversations and the comfort, I did something that honestly scared me.
I asked you for a movie.
Not because it was just a movie… but because I didn’t know how you would react. I didn’t know what this was yet. I didn’t know if I was reading things wrong. I only knew that I wanted more time with you.
And that was enough to make me nervous.
The movie came and went.
But what stayed was the feeling after it.
Something felt different.
Not louder. Not dramatic.
Just… peaceful.
For the first time, I felt like I wasn’t trying. I wasn’t pretending. I wasn’t searching for the right words.
I was simply there.
With you.
And somewhere after that day, a thought quietly settled inside me— Maybe this is what home feels like.
Not a place.
Not a moment.
A person.
And for the first time… that person started looking a lot like you.
